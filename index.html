Inspired by the Enneagram

Select the extent to which you identify with each of the statements below, with 1 being "strongly disagree" and 5 being "strongly agree."


1) "Ennui" is the word that best summarizes my outlook on life.

2) People would describe me as easygoing and unflappable.

3) Give me a staircase into the unknown and I will be the first to climb it: I long to see the world!

4) I take great pride in my appearance and coiffure, and I enjoy helping others look their best (usually by offering unsolicited haircuts).

5) Sometimes at twilight, I am seized by such unbridled joy that I need to sprint around in circles.

6) All I really need to be content with my life is a soft pillow.

7) I crave constant cuddles and kisses and affection.

8) Tomatoes are mystifying.

9) I must always have the last word in an argument, and I'm not above a little hair-pulling to get my way.

10) I love you, don't touch me.

11) I have psychic abilities: every so often I enter an involuntary trance and commune with my ancestors by making bird noises.

12) When overwhelmed by emotion, I cope by expressing myself through art (which usually takes the form of chewing plastic).

13) I am frequently irked by the childish shenanigans of my companions.

14) I am introverted and generally rather quiet (some would say sullen), but when I really want something I know how to make myself heard, usually by shrieking for lettuce at 6 am.

15) I am always the last one picked for team sports.

Type 1: The Edith

The rational, easygoing type: agreeable, self-controlled, adaptive, and complacent

Basic Desire: Hay

Basic Fear: Ramps

Ediths are cool as cucumbers under most circumstances, and very little can disturb their equanimity. Healthy Ediths are accepting, trusting, and stable; unhealthy Ediths can be obstinate, defensive, and resistant to growth and change. Ediths prefer to observe the world around them rather than enter the fray. They are easy to please and quite content to do things as they have always done them, preferring to sleep in the same spot, eat the same food, and socialize with the same friends. When their familiar routine is threatened, they can react with surprising hostility; but for the most part, Ediths are even-keeled and reliable. They prioritize comfort, consistency, and calm. They also serve as anchors for the more emotionally volatile Guinneagram types, and the unlikely friendship that sometimes forms between an Edith and a Mabel is an important stabilizing force in the Mabel's life. If you identify as an Edith, you may have been "The Teacher's Pet" in high school.

Type 2: The Mabel

The sensitive, withdrawn type: expressive, pensive, dramatic, and tempermental

Basic Desire: Lettuce

Basic Fear: Lack of lettuce

Mabels are emotional, introspective, and melancholy. They occasionally lapse into periods of nihilism, wondering what really is the point of life, but they usually abandon such existential ponderings when lettuce is made available. A healthy Mabel is thoughtful, intuitive, and inclined towards the spiritual; an unhealthy Mabel can fall prey to melodrama, self-pity, and self-indulgence. Mabels are often homebodies, but they enjoy exploring and expanding their horizons when given the opportunity. They are selective about their companions yet extremely loyal once they have identified a true friend; they tend to love Ediths in their own curmudgeonly way, although the friendship is not without its occasional squabbles. If you identify as a Mabel, you may have been "The Emo Kid" in high school. Of the five types, the Mabel is most likely to experience other-worldly phenomena and commune with The Great Beyond.

Type 3: The Lola

The curious, cerebral type: perceptive, innovative, isolated, and anxious

Basic Desire: Cucumbers

Basic Fear: Excessive cuddling

Lolas are alert, insightful, and individualistic. They constantly seek to expand their knowledge and understand their surroundings, and they are often the first to take the plunge into the unknown. However, they can also be wary of the world and its workings. A healthy Lola is observant, exploratory, imaginative, and capable of intense focus; an unhealthy Lola is easily overwhelmed by anxiety and sometime beset by nervous tics, such as gnawing on cage bars. Not comfortable with ostentatious displays of emotion, Lolas spend most of their time alone. It is possible to slowly and gently earn their trust, but expect it to take at least a year before a Lola is willing to cuddle with you. Lolas are utterly uninterested in petty social drama, and because of this they can seem a bit aloof to their peers. They are also quite shy, passive, and susceptible to bullying by the more dominant types, particularly Hesters. If you identify as a Lola, you may have been “The Nerd” in high school.

Type 4: The Hester

The optimistic, fun-loving type: spontaneous, confident, willful, and confrontational

Basic Desire: Room to zoom

Basic Fear: "The Sky Hand"

Hesters are extroverted, bold, adventurous, and occasionally domineering. Of all the types, Hesters are uniquely capable of experiencing pure, unadulterated joy, which they often express by sprinting in circles and wildly popcorning. Healthy Hesters are cheerful, exuberant, and happy-go-lucky; unhealthy Hesters are pugnacious and pushy. Hesters don’t think too deeply about why things are the way they are; they are content to merely bask in the simple pleasures of life. Hesters can be hot-tempered and they often bully the more passive Guinneagram types, but their nature is such that they almost immediately get forget what they were fighting about and want to be friends again. Hesters are usually quite strong and atheletic, and if you identify as a Hester, you may have been “The Jock” in high school. Hesters are primarily characterized by the simplicity and purity of their emotions, and though they lash out when irritated, they are fundamentally very good-natured.

Type 5: The Baahbara

The emotional, high-strung type: demonstrative, empathic, mercurial, and histrionic

Basic Desire: Chin and ear scritchies

Basic Fear: Haircuts

The Baahbara's distinguishing trait is the ability to feel things more deeply than most, and this quality is both a blessing and a curse. A healthy Baahbara is earnest, compassionate, and affectionate; an unhealthy Baahbara is needy, erratic, and melodramatic. Baahbaras approach everything in life with a characteristic intensity. Their experience of every emotion -- from joy to fear, from impatience to contentment -- is unusually heightened. Whether they are eating so quickly that they choke or drinking half their weight in water in one sitting, Baahbaras treat each day as though it were their last day on earth. This unrelenting intensity sometimes makes Baahbaras cranky and irritable, but they also have a great capactiy for empathy. They can often be found comforting and caressing their loved ones, perhaps because they themselves are so sensitive to emotions and receptive to affection. Baahbaras also tend to be a bit vain about their appearance (in all fairness, they usually have great hair). If you identify as a Baahbara, you may have been “The Prom Queen” in high school.
